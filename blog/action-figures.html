Creating Custom Action Figures from Cast Photos with Generative AI, Blender, and 3D Printing

I created a set of custom action figures based on front-facing photos taken of every one of our cast members. Here's a break down how I pulled it off. Step 1: Start with a Good Photo

On shoot day, I had club historians take front-facing photos of each cast member in a neutral pose. These images are the foundation of everything that follows, so good lighting, an empty background, and clarity help.

Step 3: Cleanup and Sculpting in Blender

Once I had the GLB files (the 3D mesh format), I imported them into Blender, a free and powerful 3D modeling software.

In Blender, I cleaned up the auto-generated meshes by:

  • Merging overlapping vertices
  • Closing non-manifold holes using the 3D Print Toolbox addon
  • Hand-sculpting missing or unclear details (for example, the restaurant logo on one of the aprons)

Step 4: Preparing for 3D Printing

After finishing the models, I exported them as .STL files and brought them into Bambu Studio, which is the slicer software for Bambu Labs 3D printers (but other slicers like Cura would work too).

At around 3.75 inches (93mm) tall (the size of a classic Kenner Star Wars figure), each figure used about 25g of PLA filament and took ~30 mins to print, including supports. For reference, a 1kg roll of PLA costs around $20, so itâ€™s pretty affordable per figure.
